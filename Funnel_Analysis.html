1. Funnel map

Build two funnels, not one

In consumer electronics, many users research online and buy offline. So the correct model is: direct online checkout funnel plus assisted lead-to-store funnel. Strong e-commerce funnel design shows how top-funnel quality, PDP intent, and bottom-funnel friction all matter; this project extends that logic to visits, demos, EMI, and installation.

What modern e-commerce teaches you

Modern e-commerce best practices show that real funnel work is about removing friction at each stage: improve discovery, improve product-card CTR, improve PDP-to-ATC, simplify cart/checkout, and reduce post-order confusion. In their deck, mWeb conversion is low, PDP-to-ATC varies strongly by device, and post-order reassurance matters because users still need confidence after payment.
Friction removal Device-specific journeys Trust after purchase Intent segmentation

What electronics needs

Electronics has a stronger need for price visibility, EMI clarity, installation confidence, warranty reassurance, delivery date certainty, and human assistance. That means your funnel should expose those answers before the user reaches checkout or leaves to compare elsewhere.

2. Benchmark

What Amazon, Flipkart, Croma, and Vijay Sales do well

The goal is not to copy their scale. The goal is to copy the trust mechanisms that make conversion easier: reviews, EMI, delivery certainty, installation clarity, support access, and demonstrations.

Player Main conversion strength What users get instantly How you should copy it
Amazon Convenience + logistics No-cost EMI, fast delivery, rich product page structure, reviews/ratings, and service visibility. Show EMI on the hero card, not only at payment; show expected install timing and warranty help on every high-ticket page.
Flipkart Discovery + comparison Reviews, ratings, comparison, EMI, pay-later, and installation/demo listings. Add compare mode, image-based proof, and a dedicated installation/demo CTA to the PDP.
Croma Support + store bridge Store locator, pincode-aware buying, EMI options, support, returns, and demo/installation help. Make your online channel a store bridge: “reserve online, visit offline, close in store, install next.”
Vijay Sales Consultative selling EMI options, bank offers, and product demonstrations. Use consultative flows: “best for 2BHK”, “best for low electricity bill”, “best for hard water”, “best for family room”.

Trust ingredients you need on every product page

Price + EMI calculator Delivery / install ETA Warranty summary Pincode stock check Return / cancellation policy Call / WhatsApp CTA Real review snippets Compare products

For high-ticket appliances, “can I trust this seller?” matters almost as much as price. That is why local assistance and clear service guarantees often outperform a pure marketplace listing.

Why offline often wins

Offline wins because it removes uncertainty in one conversation: the buyer sees the product, checks dimensions, hears the service promise, gets EMI approved, and leaves with a person to call. Online often fails because each of those questions sits on a different page or is hidden until the user is already about to drop off.

4. Experiment backlog

What to test first

Start with the experiments that most directly reduce uncertainty and increase lead capture. A good funnel test should change one anxiety at a time, not everything at once.

Priority 1

  • Sticky WhatsApp / call button on product pages.
  • EMI calculator shown on listing cards and PDP.
  • Delivery + installation ETA shown before checkout.
  • One-tap store reservation / callback.

Priority 2

  • Compare products by 3–5 buyer-friendly attributes.
  • Trust blocks: warranty, service, exchange, and installation policy.
  • Photo reviews and “sold / installed in Jaipur” proof.
  • Price transparency with offer breakdown.

Priority 3

  • Lead scoring and callback routing by category.
  • Post-install review request and referral trigger.
  • Access to extended warranty / AMC at checkout.
  • Personalized recommendations for accessories and upsells.

5. 90-day roadmap

Build the funnel before you scale traffic

The fastest way to waste ad spend is to drive traffic into a funnel that is not instrumented. This roadmap prioritizes measurement, trust, and conversion before paid growth.

Days 1–30
Instrument and simplify
  • Define every funnel event.
  • Build dashboards for lead and sale stages.
  • Place EMI, delivery, install, and warranty above the fold.
  • Launch sticky call / WhatsApp.
Days 31–60
Optimize trust and choice
  • Add compare tool and product shortlist.
  • Create category-specific pages.
  • Test proof blocks and review snippets.
  • Add callback SLAs for leads.
Days 61–90
Scale what closes
  • Double down on winning categories.
  • Retarget lead non-closers.
  • Promote AMC, warranty, and accessories.
  • Build referral loops and post-install review flow.

What not to do in Year 1

Do not hide the price story

Users need the EMI, offer, and final payable amount early.

Do not force too many steps

One-click call or WhatsApp should be available everywhere.

Do not launch broad traffic first

Fix the funnel before spending on scale.

Do not ignore post-sale

Installation, warranty, and review loops drive future sales.
